PARENTAL LEAVE AND FAMILY SUPPORT POLICY
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Document Version: 2025.1

PAID PARENTAL LEAVE

Primary Caregiver Leave:
- 16 weeks (112 days) fully paid leave
- 100% base salary continuation
- Includes birth mothers and primary adoptive parents
- May be taken continuously or split within 12 months

Secondary Caregiver Leave:
- 8 weeks (56 days) fully paid leave
- 100% base salary continuation
- For non-birth parents and secondary adoptive parents
- May be taken continuously or split within 12 months

Eligibility:
- All regular full-time and part-time (20+ hours) employees
- No minimum service requirement
- Applies to birth, adoption, foster placement, or surrogacy

LEAVE TIMING AND FLEXIBILITY

Start Date Options:
- Birth mothers: Up to 4 weeks before due date
- Non-birth parents: Anytime within 12 months of birth/placement
- Adoption: From placement date forward

Flexible Usage:
- Take all weeks consecutively
- Split into separate periods (minimum 1-week increments)
- Combine with PTO for extended leave
- Transition back gradually with part-time schedule

Return-to-Work Schedule:
- First 2 weeks: 50% schedule allowed (same pay)
- Weeks 3-4: 75% schedule allowed
- Week 5+: Full-time return
- Extended part-time requires manager approval

PREGNANCY DISABILITY LEAVE

Short-Term Disability:
- Begins when leave starts (typically 6-8 weeks for birth mothers)
- 100% salary continuation
- Medically certified recovery period
- Separate from parental bonding leave

Complications:
- Extended recovery time as medically necessary
- Covered under disability benefits
- May extend total leave period
- Doctor's certification required

Bed Rest:
- Paid leave if ordered by physician
- Remote work options if medically cleared
- Regular doctor's notes required
- Transitions to maternity leave at birth

ADOPTION AND SURROGACY

Adoption Leave:
- Same benefits as birth parents
- Applies to domestic and international adoptions
- Children up to age 18
- Foster-to-adopt situations included

Pre-Adoption Leave:
- Up to 5 days paid for court proceedings, travel, placement meetings
- Does not count toward parental leave
- Requires documentation

Surrogacy:
- Intended parents receive full parental leave benefits
- Surrogate mothers covered under pregnancy disability
- Legal documentation required

FERTILITY TREATMENT SUPPORT

Coverage Benefits:
- Up to $25,000 lifetime maximum for fertility treatments
- IVF, IUI, fertility medications covered
- 3 cycles maximum
- Includes storage fees for first 2 years

Time Off for Treatments:
- Flexible scheduling for appointments
- Up to 40 hours paid time (not from PTO)
- Same-day leave approval for procedures
- Partner may accompany for major procedures

Egg Freezing:
- Elective egg freezing: 50% covered up to $10,000
- Medically necessary: 100% covered
- Storage: Company pays first 3 years

PREGNANCY LOSS SUPPORT

Miscarriage/Stillbirth Leave:
- Up to 3 weeks paid leave
- Available to birth mother and partner
- Counseling services provided
- May request extended unpaid FMLA

Bereavement and Counseling:
- Dedicated grief counselors available
- 6 sessions fully covered (beyond standard EAP)
- Support groups facilitated by HR
- Financial planning support if needed

CHILD CARE SUPPORT

Backup Child Care:
- 20 days per year subsidized backup care
- Emergency situations: $5 copay per day
- Registered providers or in-home care
- Book through work-life platform

Child Care FSA:
- Up to $5,000 per year pre-tax
- Dependent care expenses eligible
- Day care, preschool, before/after school programs
- Summer camps (day camps only)

On-Site Benefits:
- Lactation rooms at all office locations
- Private, comfortable space with refrigeration
- Hospital-grade pump available
- Flexible break schedule for pumping

GRADUAL RETURN TO WORK

Phase-Back Program:
Weeks 1-2: 20 hours/week (50% pay)
Weeks 3-4: 30 hours/week (75% pay)
Week 5+: Full-time (100% pay)

Benefits During Phase-Back:
- Full health insurance coverage maintained
- 401(k) contributions continue
- PTO accrues at normal rate
- Performance reviews not affected

Remote Work Option:
- First 8 weeks post-return: 100% remote available
- Weeks 9-16: Hybrid schedule
- Requires manager approval
- Permanent remote considered case-by-case

NURSING AND LACTATION

Lactation Breaks:
- Reasonable breaks as needed (typically 20-30 minutes, 3 times daily)
- Paid breaks during first 6 months
- Extended breaks available unpaid after 6 months
- No requirement to make up time

Lactation Rooms:
- Available at every office location
- Private, locking rooms
- Comfortable seating, table, electrical outlets
- Refrigerator for breast milk storage
- Cleaning supplies provided

Breast Pump Coverage:
- Hospital-grade pump provided at no cost through insurance
- Or $300 reimbursement for personal pump purchase
- Accessories and replacement parts covered

Milk Shipping:
- Company covers shipping breast milk home from business travel
- Cooler and shipping materials provided
- Coordination through travel team

PARTNER/CO-PARENT SUPPORT

Paternity Leave:
- 8 weeks paid leave (secondary caregiver)
- May be split or taken consecutively
- Flexible scheduling within first year
- No "use it or lose it" - full 8 weeks available

Same-Sex Couples:
- Same benefits regardless of relationship to child
- Primary/secondary designation by parents
- Adoption process support included
- All family-building methods covered

Non-Traditional Families:
- Domestic partners receive same benefits
- Legal guardianship situations considered
- Foster parents eligible
- Step-parent adoptions included

INTERNATIONAL EMPLOYEES

Country-Specific Policies:
- Comply with local statutory requirements
- Enhanced by company benefits where allowed
- Minimum global standard: 16 weeks paid for primary caregiver
- Coordination with local benefits team required

Expatriate Parents:
- US policy applies if more generous than local
- Travel support for delivery or adoption
- Repatriation assistance if needed
- Tax equalization for leave period

BENEFITS CONTINUATION

During Paid Leave:
- All benefits continue at active employee rates
- Health, dental, vision - no interruption
- Life and disability insurance maintained
- 401(k) match continues
- PTO accrues normally

During Unpaid Leave (if extended beyond paid period):
- Benefits continue for 12 weeks total (FMLA)
- Employee responsible for premium payments
- 401(k) contributions may continue if desired
- No PTO accrual during unpaid period

JOB PROTECTION

Position Guarantee:
- Same or equivalent role upon return
- Same pay and benefits
- Same shift and location (unless agreed otherwise)
- No retaliation for taking leave

Layoff Protection:
- 6-month protection from layoffs after return
- Exception: Company-wide reductions
- Severance enhanced if laid off within 12 months

Performance Reviews:
- Leave period not counted negatively
- Prorated goals if return mid-cycle
- No impact on raise or bonus eligibility

FINANCIAL PLANNING

Parental Leave Calculator:
- Online tool estimates pay during leave
- Tax implications explained
- Budget planning resources
- One-on-one financial counseling available

Dependent Benefits:
- Add newborn to insurance within 30 days
- Special enrollment period
- Life insurance beneficiary updates
- 529 College Savings Plan enrollment

SUPPORT RESOURCES

Parent Support Groups:
- New parent meetups (monthly)
- Working parent resource groups
- Lactation consultant access
- Parenting workshops and webinars

Employee Assistance Program:
- Counseling for postpartum depression
- Family adjustment support
- Parenting coaching
- 24/7 hotline

Return-to-Work Mentorship:
- Matched with another parent who returned from leave
- Monthly check-ins for first 6 months
- Tips for work-life balance
- Career continuity planning

REQUESTING LEAVE

Notification Timeline:
- 30 days advance notice preferred
- Earlier notification for planning purposes encouraged
- Emergency situations: Notify as soon as possible

Required Documentation:
- Doctor's note with due date (for birth)
- Adoption placement papers
- Surrogacy agreement
- Birth certificate (after birth)

Approval Process:
1. Notify manager and HR
2. Complete leave request form
3. HR confirms eligibility and pay continuation
4. Maintain contact for any updates
